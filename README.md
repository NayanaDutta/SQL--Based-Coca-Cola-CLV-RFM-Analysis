# SQL-Based-Coca-Cola-CLV-RFM-Analysis
Project Overview
The primary objectives of this analysis are to:
Analyze Customer Behavior: Evaluate purchasing patterns and frequency to understand consumer habits. 
Perform RFM Segmentation: Use Recency, Frequency, and Monetary metrics to categorize the customer base.  
Calculate Customer Lifetime Value (CLV): Identify the most profitable long-term consumer segments.  
Identify Top Products: Determine which product categories drive the majority of revenue.  
Key Insights
Consumer Shifts: There is a notable growing shift toward health-conscious consumption, with Juices and Plant-Based beverages generating the highest revenue. 
Payment Preferences: Digital and cashless payment methods, specifically Debit Card and Apple Pay, generated the highest transaction values. 
Retention Opportunities: RFM analysis successfully identified high-value loyal customers and those at risk of churn, highlighting clear opportunities for targeted retention strategies. 
Revenue Drivers: A small percentage of "power users" purchase significantly more frequently than the average customer base.  
Tools Used
SQL: Used for data extraction, joining multiple tables (Customers, Orders, Products, Payments), and performing complex analytical queries such as NTILE scoring for RFM segments .  
Database Management: The project utilized structured data schemas to manage order details and payment statuses.  
Business Intelligence: Analytical thinking was applied to transform raw queries into strategic insights regarding revenue optimization and product performance.
